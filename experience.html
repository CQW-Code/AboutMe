Experience

My first job was as a Communication Specialist working with deaf and nonverbal residents of a residential facility for intellectually disabled children and adults. It was here I got into computers more (along with my first computer as a college graduation gift). I later became a Case Manager taking on most of the deaf and non-verbal clients in the building I worked in, then changed to a different set- those with dual diagnoses- mental illness AND intellectual disability- teenagers too!


I then left that job and attended The Chubb Institute in Pennsylvania- back then that was considered a "boot camp"- 7 month program in learning COBOL, C++ and Visual Basic 4.


After that I became a Quality Assurance tester working on Mainframe COBOL applications and CICS screens. I moved around to many states- Colorado, Georgia, Arizona and South Carolina and in many industries- health insurance, MCI World Com and even a short stint with FireVine testing web pages.


I'm also a graduate of DevPoint Labs based in Salt Lake City, UT. I picked up many new skills including Ruby, Ruby on Rails, React, Git, Jest, RSpec and refreshers on HTML/CSS, javascript(more like a "oh no...") and SQL (my favorite). With these skills and my past experience with Mainframe/COBOL/CICS and QA Testing I hope to find a rewarding and challenging position.
That sounded a bit trite didn't it? Seriously though, I'm not one to shy away from learning new skills and whatever else I find interesting. Learning is a life long pursuit and a passion of mine. It's with this desire that I want to get back into the "job market"!
